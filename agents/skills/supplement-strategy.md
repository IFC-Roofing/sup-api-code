# Supplement Strategy — Tribal Knowledge

## The Game: What We're Actually Doing
Insurance pays to restore the property to pre-loss condition. Our job is to make sure they pay for EVERYTHING that's actually needed — nothing more, nothing less. We don't inflate, we don't fabricate, we don't guess. We document, we justify, and we push for what's defensible.

## What to Fight For (Always)

### High-Value, High-Win-Rate Items
1. **Starter strip** — Almost always forgotten by adjusters. Separate material, separate labor rate. EV drip edge LF = starter LF. Strong defensible position.
2. **Drip edge** — If felt runs under drip edge (photo evidence), must replace. Full eaves + rakes LF.
3. **Waste on shingles** — EV suggested waste %. If carrier uses less, push for EV's calculation.
4. **Steep/high roof charges** — Carriers frequently undercount. EV shows pitch per facet. Every SQ on steep sections gets the charge.
5. **Chimney flashing** — Will be damaged during tear-off regardless. Production necessity argument.
6. **Ice & water shield** — Code requirement in most TX jurisdictions. Eaves + valleys.
7. **Pipe jack R&R + prime & paint** — If previously painted, must restore to pre-loss. Photo evidence of paint.
8. **Gutter D&R** — If gutters are screwed into drip edge and felt is under drip edge, gutters MUST come off to do the work. Photo evidence required.

### Bid Replacement Strategy
- When Xactimate can't capture complexity → get a market bid
- Common bid replacements: specialty gutters (LeafGuard, half-round), chimney caps, standing seam accent pieces, discontinued garage doors, custom stucco, window screens (FoGlass)
- F9 must explain WHY Xactimate is insufficient — complexity, custom work, discontinued materials

## What NOT to Fight For

### Red Flags — Remove Before Sending
- Items with no photo evidence AND no production/code justification
- Obvious non-storm damage
- Items that are clearly "nice to have" vs required
- Quantities that exceed EagleView with no explanation
- Anything that looks like padding — carriers flag patterns

### Low Priority — Include but Don't Die on the Hill
- Minor dollar items (<$50) with weak evidence
- Items where carrier is already paying close to EV qty
- Pricelist differences (handled via backend negotiation, not F9s)

## Phase 2 Strategy (Post-Response)

### When Carrier Approves
- Acknowledge what was approved
- Calculate momentum: how much closer are we to our target?

### When Carrier Denies
1. **Read their denial reason carefully** — address it directly in rewritten F9
2. **Don't repeat the same F9** — if they denied it once, the same language won't work twice
3. **Escalation path:** F9 rewrite → phone call (Cathy) → reinspection → appraisal
4. **Action tags per denied item:**
   - KEEP_AND_DEFEND — evidence is strong, push as-is
   - REWRITE_F9 — address their specific denial
   - NEEDS_MORE_PHOTOS — denial is evidence-based
   - ALIGN_TO_EAGLEVIEW — denial is measurement-based
   - LOW_LIKELIHOOD_BUT_KEEP — weak position, still worth including

### Denial Rebuttal Pattern
1. State what they denied and their reason (use their language)
2. Reframe with evidence
3. Connect to production necessity or code requirement
4. Reference specific attachments

## @supplement Tag Strategy
These are INTERNAL strategies that NEVER appear in carrier-facing documents:
- "Asking for trade X but money goes to trade Y" — carrier doesn't need to know our allocation
- "Requesting D&R of gutters because roof requires it" — frames gutter work as roof production necessity
- "Including stucco because removing flashing requires stucco repair" — chains scope dependencies

## The Carrier's Perspective
Adjusters look for:
1. **Documentation quality** — clean estimates, clear photos, organized attachments
2. **Reasonable quantities** — aligned with EV, not padded
3. **Specific justification** — generic F9s get ignored, specific ones get approved
4. **Code references** — IRC sections carry weight
5. **Production logic** — "we can't do A without also doing B" is compelling
6. **Patterns** — if every IFC supplement has the same 20 added items, they'll push back

## O&P Strategy
- Always applicable when 3+ trades are coordinated
- F9 references: physical office, project managers, coordinators, insurance claim specialists, warranty management
- O&P is 20% on top of trade line items
- Some carriers push back on O&P — boilerplate F9 addresses this
